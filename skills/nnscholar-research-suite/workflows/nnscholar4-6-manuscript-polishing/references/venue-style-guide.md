# Venue Style Guide

Use this file only when the user asks for target journal/conference adaptation.

## General Biomedical Journal

- Prefer concise declarative sentences.
- Keep Methods reproducible and conservative.
- Use structured abstract when requested.
- Avoid overstated clinical impact without validation.

## High-Impact Interdisciplinary Journal

- Emphasize broad question and conceptual advance.
- Keep technical detail sufficient but not crowded in the main narrative.
- State claim boundaries clearly.

## AI / Computer Science Conference

- Highlight problem, method, benchmarks, baselines, ablations, and reproducibility.
- Preserve dataset and metric names.
- Avoid vague claims such as "significantly better" without statistical support.

## Materials / Chemistry Journal

- Emphasize synthesis, characterization, structure-property relationship, and testing conditions.
- Preserve units and instrument settings.

## Social Science / Education / Psychology

- Make construct definitions and measurement logic explicit.
- Preserve identification/causal language boundaries.

## Humanities

- Prioritize argument clarity, evidence transitions, and conceptual precision.
- Do not force empirical reporting language onto interpretive work.
